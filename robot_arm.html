📅 Oct – Dec 2025 👥 Team of 4 🏆 First Year Engineering Design

Designed and built a universal end-effector for a Q-Arm robotic system capable of grabbing, lifting, and moving objects of varying shapes. Led a team as project manager, overseeing CAD design, prototyping, and a full Python codebase for automated item retrieval.

CAD Design Python 3D Printing Project Management Rack & Pinion
01

Overview

This project was the culmination of our first-year engineering design sequence at McMaster University. The goal was to design and build a universal end-effector — essentially a robotic "hand" — that could attach to a Q-Arm robotic system and reliably interact with objects of various shapes, sizes, and materials.

The project spanned the full engineering design cycle: requirements analysis, concept generation, CAD modeling, FEA simulation, prototyping through 3D printing, and software integration with Python. As project manager, I coordinated the team's efforts across mechanical, electrical, and software disciplines, ensuring milestones were met and design decisions were grounded in engineering principles.

The final prototype successfully demonstrated the ability to grasp cylindrical, rectangular, and irregularly shaped objects with consistent force application, meeting all project requirements.

02

Design & Build

⚙️

Mechanical Design

The end-effector uses a rack-and-pinion mechanism to actuate parallel gripper jaws, providing symmetric and repeatable gripping motion.

  • Rack & pinion actuation — linear motion from rotary input
  • Parallel jaw configuration for centered grip
  • Compliant gripping surfaces for varied object shapes
  • Mounting interface compatible with Q-Arm flange
💻

CAD & Simulation

Full assembly modeled in CAD with interference checking, stress analysis, and motion simulation before fabrication.

  • Complete CAD assembly with all components
  • FEA stress analysis on critical load-bearing parts
  • Motion simulation for gripper kinematics
  • Iterative design refinements based on simulation
🔌

Electronics & Control

Integrated servo motor control with position feedback and force sensing for precise and adjustable gripping force.

  • Servo motor with position feedback
  • Force sensing for adaptive grip pressure
  • Microcontroller-based control interface
  • Python control software for automated operation

Outcome

The end-effector was successfully demonstrated in a final presentation, where it picked up and relocated a variety of test objects — including a water bottle, a tennis ball, and a machined aluminum part — without dropping or damaging any of them. The project received strong feedback from faculty evaluators on its robust mechanical design and well-documented engineering process.
