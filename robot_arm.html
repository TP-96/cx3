First Year Engineering Design Project 1 · Oct – Dec 2025

Robot Arm
End-Effector

A universal end-effector for a Q-Arm robotic system capable of grabbing, lifting, and moving objects of varying shapes. Built from scratch with CAD design, 3D printing, and a full Python control system.

⏱ Oct – Dec 2025 🏫 McMaster University 👥 Team of 5
01

Overview

Led a team of five as project manager to design and build a universal end-effector for a Q-Arm robotic system. The gripper was required to handle objects of varying shapes — from cylindrical cans to irregular blocks — using a single interchangeable mechanism.

The project spanned the full engineering design cycle: requirements analysis, concept generation, CAD modeling, FEA simulation, prototyping through 3D printing, and software integration with Python.

Design & Build

Mechanical Design

The end-effector uses a rack-and-pinion mechanism driven by a single servo motor, enabling parallel jaw gripping with self-centering action. Key design decisions included:

  • Rack & Pinion Drive — ensures synchronized jaw movement and high repeatability
  • Interchangeable Jaws — custom profiles for cylindrical, rectangular, and irregular objects
  • Compliant Padding — silicone grip surfaces to prevent damage to delicate objects
  • Force Limiting — mechanical stops to prevent over-gripping and servo stall

CAD & Simulation

All components were modelled in SolidWorks with 30+ parts in the final assembly. FEA simulations validated the structural integrity under maximum grip load (2 kg). Iterative design reduced part count by 40% from the initial concept.

Electronics & Control

The gripper is controlled by an Arduino Nano running a Python-based state machine. Communication with the Q-Arm happens over serial, with a custom protocol for position, force, and grip status feedback. The system supports automatic object detection using a limit switch array embedded in the jaw faces.

03

Outcome

The final prototype achieved 95% success rate across 50 automated pick-and-place cycles with objects ranging from 20mm to 80mm in diameter. The project received top marks in the engineering design showcase.

All CAD files, Python code, and documentation are available for future teams to build upon.
