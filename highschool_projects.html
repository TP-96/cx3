Precision Aluminum Mallet

A mallet head and handle turned entirely on a manual lathe. The project required working with 6061 aluminum stock, performing facing, turning, boring, and threading operations to create an assembled tool held together by an interference fit and set screw.

  • Material: 6061 Aluminum — chosen for machinability and corrosion resistance
  • Operations: Facing, rough turning, finish turning, boring, internal threading, knurling, parting
  • Tolerances: ±0.005" on critical mating surfaces
  • Key Learning: Tool wear compensation, cutting speed optimization, surface finish control
Manual Lathe Metal Machining Aluminum Precision Turning

Maple Finger Lap Joint Box

A finely crafted storage box made from solid maple, featuring through finger (box) joints cut on a table saw using a dado stack. Every joint was hand-fitted to achieve a seamless appearance with no visible gaps.

  • Material: Solid maple — selected for its hardness, stability, and figure
  • Joinery: Through finger joints cut with dado stack on table saw, hand-fitted with chisel work
  • Finish: Danish oil followed by paste wax — bringing out maple's natural warmth
  • Key Learning: The importance of consistent glue-up pressure and how even 0.01mm errors compound over multiple fingers
